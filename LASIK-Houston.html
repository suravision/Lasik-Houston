Understanding LASIK Eye Surgery

LASIK eye surgery Houston (Laser-Assisted in Situ Keratomileusis) is a trusted and effective procedure designed to correct refractive errors such as nearsightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism. By reshaping the cornea, this advanced surgery ensures that light focuses accurately on the retina, providing sharp, clear vision.

Procedure Overview 

Performed by expert ophthalmologists in LASIK Houston, the procedure follows these precise steps:

Preparation: A thin, circular flap is created in the cornea using either a microkeratome blade or a femtosecond laser.
Reshaping: The flap is gently lifted to access the cornea. An excimer laser removes tiny amounts of tissue to reshape the cornea and correct refractive errors.
Repositioning: The flap is carefully repositioned, acting as a natural bandage that heals securely without the need for stitches.

About Houston

Houston (/ˈhjuːstən/; HEW-stən) is the most populous city in Texas and the largest in the Southern United States. Located in Southeast Texas near Galveston Bay and the Gulf of Mexico, it is the seat of Harris County and the central city of the Greater Houston metropolitan area. With a population of 2,314,157 as of 2023, Houston is the fourth-largest city in the U.S., following New York City, Los Angeles, and Chicago, and the sixth-largest city in North America. It ranks second in Texas, after Dallas-Fort Worth, and is the southeastern anchor of the Texas Triangle megaregion.

Houston spans 640.4 square miles (1,659 km²), making it the ninth-largest U.S. city by land area. It is the largest city in the country that is not consolidated with a county, parish, or borough. While mostly in Harris County, Houston extends into parts of Fort Bend and Montgomery counties, bordering other principal communities such as Sugar Land and The Woodlands.

Founded by land investors on August 30, 1836, at the confluence of Buffalo Bayou and White Oak Bayou (now Allen's Landing), Houston was incorporated on June 5, 1837. The city is named after Sam Houston, a former general and president of the Republic of Texas, who led Texas to independence from Mexico at the Battle of San Jacinto, located 25 miles east of the city. Houston briefly served as the capital of the Republic of Texas in the late 1830s and grew into a regional trading hub throughout the 19th century. The early 20th century brought rapid growth through the development of the port, railroad, Houston Ship Channel, and the Texas oil boom.

By the mid-20th century, Houston’s economy diversified, becoming home to the Texas Medical Center, the world’s largest concentration of healthcare and research institutions, and NASA's Johnson Space Center, home to the Mission Control Center. Houston's economy spans energy, manufacturing, aeronautics, and transportation, with the city housing the second-most Fortune 500 company headquarters of any U.S. city, after New York City.

The Port of Houston ranks as the leading U.S. port in international waterborne tonnage and second in total cargo tonnage. Nicknamed the "Bayou City," "Space City," "H-Town," and "the 713," Houston is a global hub in culture, medicine, and research. The city’s population is ethnically and religiously diverse, with a large international community. Houston is Texas’s most diverse metropolitan area and is often recognized as the most racially and ethnically diverse major city in the U.S. It is home to prominent cultural institutions such as the Houston Museum District and Houston Theater District.

What is LASIK eye surgery and how does it work?

LASIK (Laser-Assisted in Situ Keratomileusis) is a surgical procedure used to correct refractive vision problems, such as nearsightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism. During LASIK, a laser is used to reshape the cornea, allowing light to focus more precisely on the retina, which improves vision. The procedure is quick, minimally invasive, and typically offers rapid recovery.



Am I a Candidate for LASIK Surgery in Houston?

Not everyone is a suitable candidate for LASIK surgery. Here are some criteria to determine eligibility:

Age: You must be at least 18 years old, with stable vision for at least one year.

Eye Health: Your eyes should be free of conditions like severe dry eye syndrome, keratoconus, or significant scarring.

General Health: Autoimmune disorders, uncontrolled diabetes, or certain medications may affect healing and make LASIK less suitable.

Refractive Stability: Candidates should have a stable prescription for at least one year before surgery.

Corneal Thickness: Adequate corneal thickness is required to safely reshape the cornea during the procedure.

What Should I Expect During LASIK Surgery Recovery?

LASIK surgery recovery is generally quick, but there are key stages and precautions to keep in mind:

First 24 Hours: Vision may be blurry or hazy immediately after the procedure. Avoid rubbing your eyes and rest as much as possible. You’ll typically have a follow-up appointment the next day.

First Week: Use prescribed eye drops to prevent infection and dryness. Avoid strenuous activities, swimming, or exposure to dust and smoke.

First Month: Night vision issues like glare or halos may occur but should improve over time. Refrain from contact sports to avoid eye injury.
